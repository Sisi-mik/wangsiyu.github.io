My Research

Joint work

Speaking for the Silence: a Voice Splitting Analysis of Mandarin gěi

With Jack Isaac Rabinovitch, Harvard University, Ph.D student

This is a project on Voice and thematic licensing based on Mnadarin gěi, the starting point of which is to see if it is possible to propose a unviersal analysis of all the distributions of gěi. Based on their behaviors with respect to progressive aspect and subevent encoding, we argue that (i) gěi is a functional head which projects above null theta-role licensing heads; (ii) gěi forms a instantaneous event, making it incompatible with progressive, and (iii) structures in which gěi and prog co-occur contain two subevents, such that co-occuring prog and gěi target different subevents. The project is presented on the Workshop on Theoretical East Asian Linguistics 13 (TEAL), see the poster and the abstract.

Parameter Theory: historical development, latest advancement, and reflection

With Li Yingyi and Chen Yifan, Peking University, Ph.D students

This is a literature review on the Parameter Theory under the generative approach(written in Chinese). The article summaries the development of Parameter Theory from GB to MP era and identifies two main changes: the proposal of microparameter and the interface property of language parameter. Adopting the assumptions of Distributed Morphology, we argue a “distributed parametric theory”, suggesting that parameters should be located in different positions according to their properties. This paper has been submited to the Journal of Foreign Languages and is undergoing the second round of revision. See the current draft.

The argument structure of Chinese Sign Language (CSL): a preliminary investigation

With Lin Hao, Shanghai International Studies University, Lecturer and Reseacher

The study of argument structure in spoken languages has been centered on the syntactic configuration and its relation with particular lexical items. This study aims to fill in the gap by providing a systematic typological description of the CSL argument structure and argument alternations. By placing CSL in the whole typological picture of argument structure of sign languages, we hope to shed some light on the nature of argument realization by echoing back the theories on the lexical-syntax interface. However, relatively few studies systematically investigate the argument structure of sign languages. This project is still in the early stage. See the abstract submitted to ICFL 10.
